    About Me

    Hi everyone, I'm Connie!

    I'm currently a junior at Rutgers majoring in Aerospace Engineering with a minor (but maybe double major) in Physics. I am fascinated by all feats of engineering and science, from obvious ones like rocket launches and spacecraft, to more daily ones, like how people will eat more M&Ms if there's more colors.

    But specifically, I am passionate about exploring the universe we live in. Whether that exploration comes in the form of discovering fundamental laws of physics that govern our creation, or creating spacecraft to search for clues about the formation of life, I want to learn how the world works, and hopefully contribute to our discoveries in the future.

    As I slowly navigate the worlds of engineering and physics, I've had the awesome chance to do a few things that might be cool:

    • Doing a co-op as a Pathways Intern in fall 2019 at NASA GSFC working in the Power Systems branch, and have since returned for the following summer and fall semesters in the Propulsion and Flight Dynamics branches
    • Working with Prof. Burlion and STAR to develop Rutgers' first CubeSat Proposal
    • Embracing communities and mentorship with Tea Time and SoE Ambassador
    In my spare time, I love to draw! I've been drawing digitally since 2012, and you can find more of my artwork here :)! I am also a huge fan of these things: hiking, musicals, playing the violin, forever scrolling through Petfinder and wishing to adopt a cat, and K-indie.

    Thanks for dropping by! Hope you enjoy your stay!
